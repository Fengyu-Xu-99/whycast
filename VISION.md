# WhyCast Vision

WhyCast started because I noticed something about how I naturally learn.

I am an extremely curiosity-driven person. Instead of following structured courses or reading textbooks from beginning to end, my curiosity is usually triggered by something random that happens during everyday life:

- playing a video game
- watching a documentary
- seeing an interesting animal
- hearing an economic concept
- noticing a business decision
- seeing a scientific phenomenon

Those small moments make me immediately ask:

> Why?

But the first question is almost never the most interesting one.

For example, while playing *The Hunter: Call of the Wild*, I became curious about Bloodhounds.

At first my question was:

> Why are Bloodhounds so good at tracking?

After thinking for a few minutes, I realized what I was actually curious about was deeper:

- Why do Bloodhounds physically look like that?
- Why the loose skin?
- Why the giant ears?
- Why those wrinkles?
- Why does evolution create a dog that looks like this?

That single question naturally expanded into dozens of connected questions:

- What makes scent hounds different from sight hounds?
- How do hunting dogs evolve?
- What other dogs have similar abilities?
- What makes smell so powerful?
- Could technology replicate this?
- Why do humans rely on dogs instead of building machines?

My brain naturally builds a network of connected questions.

The problem is that existing AI assistants usually answer only the first question. They do not help me discover the questions I have not asked yet.

That realization became the origin of WhyCast.

## Purpose

WhyCast transforms curiosity into structured exploration.

Instead of only answering questions, it helps people explore them.

## Creator Motivation

This project is also a personal learning project.

I work professionally as a data manager and technical project lead. Most of my daily work involves planning, managing projects, documentation, and coordinating work.

Although I enjoy product thinking, I miss building software myself.

One of my personal goals is to become a stronger software builder by developing a real product from scratch. Instead of building another generic portfolio app, I want to build something I would personally use every week.

This project has two equal purposes:

1. Build a useful curiosity exploration tool.
2. Practice modern software engineering through a real product.

## Personal Motivation

One challenge I have is that I naturally overthink.

When I become interested in a topic, I often dive too deeply into one branch before organizing the bigger picture. I can spend hours researching details while forgetting the original reason I became curious.

WhyCast should become a thinking partner that helps organize curiosity before diving into details.

Instead of immediately exploring one rabbit hole, the system should first show the landscape of possible directions. Only then should I choose where to explore.

The goal is not to reduce curiosity.

The goal is to guide it.

## Long-Term Possibilities

Today, WhyCast is a coding project. Tomorrow, it could become much more:

- AI-generated curiosity maps
- Interactive learning journeys
- Personalized podcast generation
- Documentary-style storytelling
- Knowledge graph visualization
- Daily curiosity recommendations
- Community exploration
- Educational platform

These are future possibilities, not MVP requirements.

## Current Objective

Build a clean, maintainable MVP that proves the core idea:

> Curiosity becomes much more valuable when it is structured before it is answered.

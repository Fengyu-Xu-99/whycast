# Episode Workflow

This workflow is for making personal WhyCast episodes before building any app.

## Purpose

Create a repeatable process for turning the project owner's real curiosity triggers into research-backed Chinese podcast scripts.

For now, the output is a script the owner wants to read. Audio comes later.

## Folder Pattern

Each episode lives in its own folder:

```text
episodes/<episode-slug>/
```

Recommended files:

- `episode-brief.md`
- `research-notes.md`
- `script-draft-v0.md`
- `script-draft-v1.md`
- `script-draft-v2.md`
- `audio-notes.md`

## Step 1 - Brief

Capture:

- real-life trigger
- core question
- why the question matters to the owner
- first question clusters
- language and tone
- reference style
- known dislikes or constraints

Do not draft the episode yet.

## Step 2 - Research Notes

Create source notes before writing a serious script draft.

For each source, capture:

- source title
- URL
- why it is credible enough to use
- usable claims
- claims to avoid or phrase carefully

Prefer:

- official breed organizations for breed descriptions
- peer-reviewed reviews or papers for biology and mechanism
- reputable educational references for history and category framing

Avoid relying on:

- unsourced blog claims
- repeated internet trivia
- exact numbers without a strong source
- convenient explanations that sound good but are not supported

## Step 3 - Overview Script

Create a first full-shape script.

This draft can be imperfect. It should prove:

- the topic has enough depth
- the structure works
- the tone is close
- the sources support the main claims

## Step 4 - Owner Feedback

The owner reads the script before audio.

Feedback should focus on:

- too shallow
- too long or too short
- unnatural Chinese
- too much opening
- too much meta commentary
- weak or vague claims
- parts that should be expanded
- parts that should be cut

## Step 5 - Personal Style Revision

Revise using `PERSONAL_STYLE.md`.

Default revision priorities:

- deepen the explanation
- make examples concrete
- remove fluffy or generic summary
- keep uncertainty in source notes, not as repetitive narration
- make the Chinese sound spoken and natural

## Step 6 - Audio Readiness

Only after the script feels right:

- add pronunciation notes
- split long sentences
- remove awkward symbols
- generate a short audio sample
- listen for pacing and pronunciation
- then generate the full audio draft

## Done Criteria

An episode script is ready for audio when:

- the owner wants to listen to it
- the knowledge feels accurate enough to learn from
- major claims have sources
- weak claims are removed or clearly softened
- the ending is specific to the topic, not generic WhyCast framing
